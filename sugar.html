Sugar Awareness

Understanding sugars for a healthier lifestyle

What are Sugars?

Sugar is a simple carbohydrate found in various forms. Glucose and fructose join together to make sucrose, which is commonly known as 'sugar'. Sugars can be naturally occurring, like those in fruits and milk, or they can be added to different foods and drinks as 'free sugars'. Free sugars are those added to foods and drinks and also those found naturally in fruit juices, purees, honey, and syrups.

Key Facts about Sugar

  • Sugars can be naturally occurring or added to foods and drinks.
  • 'Free sugars' are those added to foods and drinks and also those found naturally in fruit juices, purees, honey, and syrups.
  • We should be eating less foods and drinks with 'free sugars'.
  • Sugary foods and drinks can have large amounts of calories, leading to weight gain and obesity.
  • In the UK, we consume more sugar than is recommended.
  • The main sources of sugar in the UK diet are sugary drinks, fruit juice, cakes, biscuits, desserts, sweet spreads, and sweets.

How does a High Intake of Sugars Affect Our Health?

How Can I Eat Less Sugar?

Reducing sugary drinks, fruit juice, cakes, biscuits, desserts, sweet spreads, and sweets in our diet may help us reduce our free sugars intake. Here are some easy sugar swaps:

  1. Swap sugary drinks for sugar-free versions or water.
  2. Swap sweet snacks for fresh fruit or something savoury like wholegrain crackers with cheese.
  3. If you eat sweet foods like cakes and chocolate, try cutting down your portion sizes.
  4. Try natural yogurt with fruit instead of yogurts with added sugar.
  5. Check labels when choosing breakfast cereals and go for those with less sugar.
  6. Swap jams or chocolate spreads for lower sugar nut butters.
  7. Keep fruit juices or smoothies to one small glass (150ml) per day.
