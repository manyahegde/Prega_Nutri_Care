What is Fat?

During pregnancy, fat is an important part of a balanced diet as it provides energy and helps in the development of your babyâ€™s brain and nervous system. It also helps your body absorb essential vitamins like A, D, E, and K.

However, it's essential to consume healthy fats and avoid excessive saturated fats which can lead to health issues.

How does Fat Affect Your Health During Pregnancy?

Consuming healthy fats during pregnancy:

  • Supports the growth and development of your baby
  • Provides essential nutrients for brain development
  • Helps regulate hormones and supports your overall health

On the other hand, excessive saturated fats can lead to:

  • Increased risk of gestational diabetes
  • Higher cholesterol levels
  • Weight gain and related complications

What Are the Different Types of Fat?

Fats in foods can either be saturated or unsaturated. Most foods that contain fat have a mixture of both saturated and unsaturated fats in different proportions. We normally describe a food as being high in saturated or unsaturated fat depending on which type they have more of.

We need to be aware of the fat in our diet. For a healthy diet, it is important that most of the fats we eat are unsaturated.

Saturated fats:

  • Red meat
  • Butter
  • Cheese
  • Processed meats
  • Coconut oil
  • Palm oil
  • Ice cream
  • Cakes, pastries, and biscuits
  • Chocolate and chocolate spreads

Unsaturated fats:

Healthy sources of unsaturated fats include:

  • Olive oil
  • Avocado
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Fatty fish like salmon and mackerel
  • Flaxseeds and chia seeds
